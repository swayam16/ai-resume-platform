Project 03 — AI-Powered Platform

Designing an AI resume
screening platform

End-to-end UX and interface design for a machine learning platform that helps recruiters screen candidates 10× faster without sacrificing quality.

FigmaFramerFastAPIAccessibility AuditUser PersonasTask Flows

The problem

Recruiters at mid-to-large companies screen 200+ resumes per open role. The process is manual, inconsistent, and biased by recency (whoever they review last tends to score highest). The client needed an AI-powered dashboard that surfaces the best candidates without creating a black box experience.

6.2h
Average screening time
Per role, before AI assistance. Recruiters reported severe decision fatigue.
61%
Inconsistent scoring
Same recruiter scored identical resumes differently on different days.
3
Rounds of usability testing
12 participants per round. Iterated from wireframes to production prototype.

Key design decisions

The core challenge was making the AI score feel trustworthy, not arbitrary. Recruiters needed to understand why a candidate scored 94 — not just that they did.

⚡ Explainable AI scores
Instead of a single opaque match score, we designed a 5-dimension breakdown: Skills match, Experience level, Culture fit signals, Education relevance, and Communication quality. Each dimension shows a bar and a 1-line explanation in plain language.
🔄 Status workflow UI
A visual pipeline from Applied → Screened → Shortlisted → Interview → Offer keeps the recruiter oriented at every stage. Single-click stage transitions with undo support. Bulk actions for screening 10+ at once.
♿ Accessibility-first
Full keyboard navigation, ARIA live regions for dynamic score updates, 4.5:1+ contrast on all text. Tested with NVDA and macOS VoiceOver. WCAG 2.1 AA certified.
⚡ Real-time candidate ranking
Scores update live as the job description changes. Framer prototype used real dummy data and animated transitions to simulate the live-ranking experience in usability tests.
